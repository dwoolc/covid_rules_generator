Cookies, Consent, You & Me

21/11/2020

Some more details on cookies and this site for the intellectually masochistic

This page serves as both a Privacy & Cookie notice, and so should cover the bases for most legislation governing privacy & data collection internationally. Happily, I'm an individual and making very limited use of cookies on this site, so we can keep this nice and short.

Just so that we're clear what we're talking about, a cookie is a small .txt file saved on a browser from a site which is then fed back to the site when interacting with it, for saving preferences and similar quality-of-life improvements. This means that it sits on the user (your) side, rather than the site side, and is fed back whenever you interact with the site and the site requests it. For example, when you consent to cookie collection, you receive a cookie identifying this consent. The site will then check for this cookie on your browser before offering you the consent banner; if you have consented you won't see it.

Cookies come in several flavours - the first types split are session and persistent. Session cookies last for your browsing session, whilst persistent cookies with "persist" on your browser beyond the session (a classic case of does what it says on the tin).

Cookies may also be first-party (placed directly by the site you're visiting), or third-party, being placed by an entity other than the site you're visiting (think marketing, advertisement and analytics). All of my cookies are first-party, so it's just you and me.

Most cookies just save preferences or track if you're logged in. There are some sophisticated setups which are able to track your browsing behaviours on other sites. To do this, they need to be persistent, and they'll generally be third party. So none of that here.

This site uses a very small number of cookies to assist in tracking user activity. There is a single cookie which monitors the single browsing session, and a second which logs whether you have consented to the collection of cookies. And that's it. They're both session cookies, meaning that they won't persist at your end once you leave the site and I'm not using them to spy on you.

Cookies are categorised based on their purpose.

  1. Strictly Necessary
  2. Preferences
  3. Statistics
  4. Marketing

The cookies this site uses all fall under Strictly Necessary, in that they're fundamental to site function.

I'm not reviewing or accessing these cookies directly, and as they're session cookies they expire at the end of the browsing session to comply with the general principle of GDPR to keep data collection to a necessary minimum, and thus also reduce your general exposure.

If you want to follow up on any of the above, or would like clarifications, feel free to hit me up via Get In Touch. You can also enjoy more riveting reading on the GDPR site.

If you're not happy at this point, we're at a bit of stalemate - you can contact me, but you'll need to implicitly consenting to giving me more data than you would do via the session cookies...
